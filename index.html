About

Biography

Antonio Ortega is Dean's Professor of Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Southern California (USC), where he has been a faculty member since 1994. He received his BS/MS in Telecommunications Engineering from Universidad Politécnica de Madrid (1989) and his Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering from Columbia University (1994).

A founding figure of Graph Signal Processing (GSP), Prof. Ortega's research spans graph-based data analysis, image and video compression, 3D point cloud processing, machine learning on graphs, and sensor networks. His book Introduction to Graph Signal Processing (Cambridge University Press, 2022) is the definitive reference in the field.

He currently serves as VP Publications of the IEEE Signal Processing Society (2024–2026) and holds a visiting position at the University of Osaka (2023–present). He is a Fellow of both IEEE (2007) and EURASIP (2018).

Expertise

Research Areas

Spanning foundational theory to cutting-edge applications across signal processing, machine learning, and data science.

📈

Graph Signal Processing

Pioneering the field of GSP — sampling theory, filter banks, graph wavelets, graph Fourier transforms, and graph learning. Author of the foundational textbook and seminal survey papers with 5,000+ citations.

🎥

Image & Video Compression

Rate-distortion optimization, graph-based transforms for video coding, content-adaptive transforms, image coding for machines, and UGC compression with denoised references. Collaborations with Google, YouTube, and Tencent.

🤖

Machine Learning on Graphs

Graph-based semi-supervised learning, active learning, deep neural network analysis using graph tools, non-negative kernel regression, and out-of-distribution detection.

📡

3D Point Cloud & Gaussian Splatting

Point cloud attribute compression, denoising, quality assessment, 3D Gaussian splatting data coding, volumetric video conferencing, and dynamic polygon clouds for VR/AR.

🛰

Sensor Networks & Remote Sensing

Distributed data compression, graph-based wavelets for sensor webs, soil moisture downscaling, satellite image fusion, and efficient data gathering with NASA and NSF support.

💡

Oilfield & Industrial Applications

Waterflood tomography, reservoir characterization, upscaling of geological models using graph wavelets, anomaly detection for sensor drift, and fault detection using GSP. Funded by Chevron and DOE.
